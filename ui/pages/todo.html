AKI Definition

↑sCr ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours

↑sCr ≥1.5 times baseline, in the past 7 days

urine output < 0.5 mL/kg/hour for 6 hours

AKI Stages

Serum Creatinine Urine Output
1 1.5-1.9 times baseline <0.5 mL/kg/hour for 6-12 hours
2 2.0-2.9 times baseline <0.5 mL/kg/hour for ≥12 hours
3 3.0 times baseline OR ↑ sCr ≥4.0 mg/dL OR RRT <0.3 mL/kg/hour for ≥24 hours or anuria for ≥12 hours

TO DO LIST:

  1. Evaluate volume status
  2. Think about SEPSIS as a possibility
  3. Stop ACEIs/ SARTANs/AINS
  4. Check aminoglycoside (GENTA/ AMIKA-) indication
  5. Revision for change in drug dosing
  6. Consider alternatives to iodine-based contrast procedures
  7. Avoid hypotension → Review antihypertensive doses

GLYCEMIC STATUS MANAGEMENT

  1. Avoid Hyperglycaemia (> 180 mg/dl)
  2. STOP oral antidiabetic agents (! Metformin → lactic acidosis)
  3. Avoid hypoglycaemia (< 80 mg/dl)

REQUEST INVESTIGATIONS:

  1. Urine exam
  2. Abdominal ultrasound
  3. Monitor creatinine and urine output DAILY
  4. Optimal fluid balance → check INPUT ↔ OUTPUT

ASK NEPHROLOGYST INTERVENTION

  1. AKI stage 2 and 3
  2. Primary renal disease
  3. AKI of unknown aetiology
  4. AKI complications requiring RRT → severe Hiper K+ or acidosis or fluid overload
